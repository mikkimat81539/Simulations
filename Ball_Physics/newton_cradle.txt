- Kinetic Energy is energy from motion
	EXAMPLE:
		- A rolling Bowling ball
		- Moving car
		- Thrown baseball
		- Swinging hammer

	- Any object in motion has kinetic energy
	- Kinetic energy depends on both mass and speed
		- depends more on the speed
	- KE = 1/2 (m*v^2)
		- m = mass
		- v = velocity

	- if mass doubles, than kinetic energy doubles
	- if speed doubles, quadruples

	EXAMPLE of kinetic energy:
		- Red ball: mass = 0.05kg v = 5 m/s
				- red ball has a smaller mass
			ren_ball KE = 0.1J

		- Green ball: mass = 0.1kg v = 2 m/s
				- green ball has a bigger mass
			green_ball KE = 0.2J

		- Green ball has more kinetic energy because it has more mass

- Elastic Collision is where total kinetic energy is conserved (the total amount of kinetic energy is constant)
	- This means it remains the same value before and after collision 

	- EXAMPLE of Elastic Collision: Kred_init + Korange_init = Kred_final + Korange_final
		- initial means before collision
		- final mean after collision
