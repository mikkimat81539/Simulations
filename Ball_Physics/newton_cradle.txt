- Kinetic Energy is energy from motion
- Kinetic Energy is the motion energy of an object
	EXAMPLE:
		- A rolling Bowling ball
		- Moving car
		- Thrown baseball
		- Swinging hammer

	- Any object in motion has kinetic energy
	- Kinetic energy depends on both mass and speed
		- depends more on the speed
	- KE equation = 1/2 (m*v^2)
		- m = mass
		- v = velocity

	- if mass doubles, than kinetic energy doubles
	- if speed doubles, quadruples

	EXAMPLE of kinetic energy:
		- Red ball: mass = 0.05kg v = 5 m/s
				- red ball has a smaller mass
			ren_ball KE = 0.1J
				J = joules

		- Green ball: mass = 0.1kg v = 2 m/s
				- green ball has a bigger mass
			green_ball KE = 0.2J

		- Green ball has more kinetic energy because it has more mass

- Momentum: The quantity of motion possessed by a body
- formula of momentum (linear):  P = m*v
	- m = mass (measured in kg)
	- v = velocity (measured in m/s)

- Momentum is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction

	- EXAMPLE of momentum: moving truck and a moving bike both have same velocity but truck has more mass, so it'll have more momentum than the bike which has less mass

- Momentum is never lost, it only transfers from one object to another

- According to the law of conservation of momentum, in a closed system where no external forces are acting, the total momentum stays constant if no external force acts
	- the total momentum of an object before a collision is equal to the total momentum of an object after the collision

- momentum is not created or destroyed

conservation of momentum formula: (m1*v1) + (m2*v2) + ... = (m1+v1') +(m2+v2') + ...
				- p_initial = p_final

- Elastic Collision is where total kinetic energy is conserved (the total amount of kinetic energy is constant)
	- This means it remains the same value before and after collision 

	- EXAMPLE of Elastic Collision: Kred_init + Korange_init = Kred_final + Korange_final
		- initial means before collision
